Using Hazen-Williams equation to determine pressure loss due to pipe friction for water
P_loss = Pressure loss due to friction (psi) - Value to calculate
L = Pipe length (chosen unit) - Will be a constant value represented by each tile
Q = Flow rate of water inside pipe (gpm) - Will be a constant value decided by group
C = Pipe coefficient - Will make assumption that all pipes are same material (assuming Aluminum -> 120)
Di = Pipe inside diameter - Will use an average constant value

P_loss is to be calculated individually for each tile in a 2-d array based on the distance of said tile from our main "resivoir" tile
P_loss will be recalculated after algorithmically placing our "pump" tile

Distance = Pipe length multiplied by tile-distance from the nearest "pump" or "resevoir"

We need our pressure loss at the furthest tile from a pump to be equal to a minimum required pressure-
So that we can calculate the required starting pump pressure needed to keep our pressure at the tile-
above the minimum value. Using this required pressure value, we calculate the energy needed and convert-
said energy into a term that can be somewhat comparable to kilowatt hours which are used for calculating-
price of energy consumption.

To find our initial starting pressure that will allow us to reach a distance with a certain minimum value-
we first must find that minimum value. For our program, we will assume average  recommended household water-
pressure, which is 50 psi. Our P_loss must be equal to 50 psi.

We will calculate our P_loss for each individual tile based on it's calculated distance from it's furthest tile.
Once our distance is calculated and our maximum P_loss is calculated, our necessary starting pressure will be
equal to our maximum P_loss + our 50 psi minimum pressure.

Using the Hydraulic Pump Power equation, our variables are as follows:
P_h(kW) is our hydraulic power in terms of kilowatts
q is our flow, this will be randomly generated and will refer to the water consumption per tile
p is our P_loss calculated per tile, converted from psi to N/m^2

Our output will be converted from Kilowatts to Kilowatt hours by multiplying our kW output by 24, as cost is calculated-
on a daily basis, and our kWh estimate will be converted to money using a constant conversion rate

This will all be calculated per tile
